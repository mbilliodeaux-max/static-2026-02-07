DETAILED OBSERVATIONS

1. Electrical System Hazards (Fire Risk)

Observation: The electrical system exhibits signs of thermal damage and unworkmanlike installation.

  • Interior receptacles show clear evidence of arcing, melting, and scorching on the faceplates. [Photo 054] [Photo 055]
  • Light switches are physically broken or missing covers, exposing internal mechanisms. [Photo 014] [Photo 056]
  • The attic contains "flying splices" (wire connections made outside of a junction box) and open cabling. [Photo 029] [Photo 033]
  • Main electrical panel shows age and lack of proper cover/labeling. [Photo 026]
  • Service entrance exterior wiring is weathered; loose cabling observed under structure. [Photo 058] [Photo 060]

2. Active Structural Damage / Wood Destroying Organisms

Observation: The wood framing members supporting the floor system show signs of biological deterioration.

  • Sub-floor framing members (sills/joists) show evidence of advanced rot and potential termite activity. [Photo 059] [Photo 061]

3. Foundation Pier Stability

Observation: The pier-and-beam foundation shows signs of movement.

  • Concrete piers in the crawlspace show signs of leaning or settling. [Photo 066]
  • Improper shimming observed (wood blocks). [Photo 064]
  • Piers sitting in water. [Photo 062]

4. Exterior Envelope (Siding & Trim)

Observation: The exterior weather barrier is compromised.

  • Moderate to severe rot on fascia boards and soffits. [Photo 000] [Photo 001]
  • Loose and missing siding planks allowing water behind the weather barrier. [Photo 002]
  • Window sills show deep rot and deterioration. [Photo 019]
  • Porch post deterioration. [Photo 018]

5. Plumbing System Corrosion

Observation: Supply and waste piping in the crawlspace is in poor condition.

  • Cast iron waste lines in the crawlspace exhibit heavy corrosion and scaling. [Photo 068] [Photo 072]
  • Potential for imminent failure/leaks in waste lines.

6. Crawlspace Drainage & Standing Water

Observation: The crawlspace is not properly drained, resulting in "lake-effect" conditions under the home.

  • Significant standing water observed throughout the crawlspace foundation area. [Photo 075] [Photo 077]
  • This compromises soil bearing capacity and promotes wood rot/mold.

7. Roof System & Attic Penetrations

Observation: The roof covering and attic ventilation require attention.

  • Visible daylight through roof eaves; excessive gaps observed. [Photo 042] [Photo 046]
  • Evidence of water intrusion staining on roof decking. [Photo 039]
  • Interior ceiling damage/stains. [Photo 004] [Photo 005]

8. Interior Cosmetic Finishes

Observation: General wear and tear and damage from settlement/leaks.

  • Damaged/bowing wall paneling. [Photo 006] [Photo 011]
  • Missing or detached door trim. [Photo 028]

9. Exterior Vegetation & Grading

Observation: Site conditions promote moisture retention near the home.

  • Dense vegetation is encroaching upon and directly contacting the exterior siding. [Photo 003]
